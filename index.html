From studying astronaut bone density loss as a NASA intern to building research hardware for ISS at Space Tango, I have always had a passion for space life sciences and aerospace medicine. I am currently a Ph.D student at the University of Colorado Boulder within the Bioastronautics program in the department of Aerospace Engineering Sciences. My research explores the use of adaptive virtual reality astronaut training as a potential countermeasure for spaceflight associated neuroplasticity. When I'm not in the lab, you can find me playing piano, taking ballet classes, or spending time with my dog, Apollo.
